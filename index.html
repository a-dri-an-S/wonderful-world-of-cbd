What is CBD?

CBD (cannabidiol) is one of several components called cannabinoids that is found in both cannabis and hemp plants. This active-ingredient has been found to have a whole range of health benefits with therapeutic applications. Unlike THC, the main psychoactive canabinoid that is found in marijuana, CBD is completely non-psychoactive and will not produce the euphoric effect that THC can bring on when consumed. For this reason, CBD has gained a significant amount of popularity as an alternative to pharmaceuticals for helping manage pain, stress and anxiety to name a few.

How does CBD work?

In order to understand how CBD works in the body, we must first take a look at our own bodies endocannabinoid system (ECS). ECS, in short, is a complex signaling system in our body that regulates a slew of biological functions. Although, there is still much needed research to fully understands all of its functionality, what we do know will help in understanding how CBD can work for us.

ECS produces its own cannabinoids(endocannabinoids) that bind to two receptors, CB1 & CB2. The CB1 receptors are mostly found in our brain, while the CB2 receptors are mostly located within our immune and gastrointestinal system. However, we do have CB1 & CB2 receptor sites all throughout the body, the main concentration is in those specified areas. Depending on where these endocannabinoids bind to these receptors, research has linked the ECS to the following biological processes:

  • metabolism
  • chronic pain
  • immune system response, such as inflammation
  • learning & memory
  • sleep
  • cardiovascular function
  • reproductive function
  • nervous system function

...to name a few!

So, when CBD enters the body, it influences cannabinoid receptors and aids in the production of our body's own endocannabinoids. It essentially lends a helping hand to ECS and ensures it keeps things running smoothly. Also, CBD has been known to interact with other receptors such as our opioid, dopamine and serotonin receptors.

How can CBD help?

Although, there is more research to be done, there is still enough evidence to show some of the benefits of supplementing with CBD.

Stress & Anxiety

There is a large amount of anecdotal evidence that claims CBD can have a relaxing affect and can aid when dealing with stress and anxiety. Some research claims this is caused by influencing the activity in our serotonin system.

Insomnia

For those looking to get better, uninterrupted sleep, CBD may be the cure for that. The research still isn't clear on how it works, but one thing is for sure that it is dose dependant and a larger dose tends to work better than a smaller dose. Another thing to keep in minds with sleep is sometimes stress and anxiety can affect the quality of sleep and the reduction in either can help in getting a better nights rets.

Pain

According to research, CBD can help with two types of pain: neuropathic (nerve damage) and inflammatory (inflammation of the body). For this reason, it is considered a good alternative to pharmaceutical pain killers. Also, since its known to bind to opioid receptors, it can help in the reduction of opioid intake,as it enhances the effects of them.

How do I take CBD?

With the ever increasing popularity of CBD, there are a number of ways one can consume or use CBD. Here are the most popular methods of using CBD:

  • smoking or vaping(quick acting, fastest onset)
  • edibles (consumed as a food or supplement, takes longer to feel the effect)
  • Sublingual Tincture (mainly extracted into oil, but alcohol is an option too)
  • Topicals and Lotions(for a more localized approach)

As you can see, there are a number of reasons to introduce CBD into your daily regimen so, why not give it a shot and try it out!
